Web Development Journey


The Author

Stephen Brooks

Prior to attending Bryan University I had learned some front end languages. I had realized that I needed to learn more with the front end and the backend to get a job as a developer. I had finished FSW 105, FSW 100 and FSW 110 so far, and then moving onto the next courses. I plan on using what I learn to improve what I know now and apply it as I move forward with this program.

Summary Some things I noticed that can be a potential road block are not being prepared for an interview, not practicing what you learned, being realistic with yourself. Making a first impression is important, but also knowing how to code according to the industry use. During my journey with Bryan University, I will continously practice so I will retain what I learn and practice what I will learn.
